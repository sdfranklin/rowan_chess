Rules

How To Play

Learn the standard game here, then choose your hidden opening and start playing.

Pieces

  • Pawns move horizontally or vertically through empty spaces, and may keep going while the path stays open.
  • Pawns capture horizontally and vertically from adjacent range. They may capture knights forwards but not backwards.
  • Knights only move forward by jumping over the piece directly in front of them and landing two places away.
  • Knights may jump over either their own pawn or an opposing piece. They may land on and take an opposing pawn, but they cannot land on an opposing knight.
  • If another forward jump is available after landing, the knight must keep jumping within the same move.
  • No piece may move diagonally.

Scoring And Winning

  • At the beginning of the game each player secretly chooses one of their five columns to be empty.
  • The home line is 5 spaces from the starting position of your knights. A knight on or beyond the home line is considered home.
  • The first player to get 2 knights home wins immediately.
  • If both players are down to 1 live knight, the player with the first of these knights home wins.
  • If a player loses all their knights, they immediately lose.
  • If a player has no legal moves, that player loses.
